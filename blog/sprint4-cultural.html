1. Reserach Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

neuroplasticity is an umbrella term referring to the ability of your brain to reorganise itself, both physically and functionally, through experiences to your environment, behaviour, thinking, and emotions. previously, scientists thought that the brain's structure was hard-wired and immutable. New research has taught us that the brain is actually plastic, it is able to change based on experiences. Even in old age, the adult brain retains neuroplasticity and is able to change its structure and functon. Neuroplasticity enables people to recover from stroke, injury, and helps create new ways of functioning and behaviour for those experiencing autisem, ADD, learning disabilities, depression and addiction and reverse obsessive-compulsive patterns.

2. Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant?

Growth Mindset means we can develop our ability through hard work and effort. Having a growth mindset (the belief that you are in control of your own ability, and can learn and improve) is the key to success.

In people with a growth mindset, the brain is most active when they are being told what they could do to improve.
People with a growth mindset view a setback as an opportunity to learn. They tend to try harder in an effort to overcome the problem.

Professor Dweck explained growth mindset as, “In a growth mindset students understand that their talents and abilities can be developed through effort, good teaching and persistence. They don’t necessarily think everyone’s the same or anyone can be Einstein, but they believe everyone can get smarter if they work at it.”

Along with encouraging a growth mindset, helping students understand that their brain can actually grow and become stronger can also boost their confidence and improve learning. Research shows us that the brain is plastic: with focus and practice, the brain can change, it can grow new cells, and we can strengthen neurologic pathways. This is called neuroplasticity, or the ability of the brain to restructure itself based on repetitive practices.

Mindsets are not just important for learning new skills. They can affect the way that we think about everything. For example, a growth mindset can help you recover from illness because you believe that you can do something about the illness. They can help you achieve in sport, at work and can also help you grow and develop in relationships. Cultivating a growth mindset could be the single most important thing you ever do to help you achieve success.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

There are three key things that we can do to develop a growth mindset:

  1. We need to recognise that a growth mindset is not just good, but is also supported by science. In other words, we need to be committed to developing a growth mindset.
  2. We can learn and teach others about how to develop and improve their abilities through adopting a growth mindset. This will help us to take control of our life, which is hugely empowering. Research shows that people who feel in control tend to perform better. It’s a virtuous cycle.
  3. Listen out for our fixed mindset voice. When we hear that little critical voice in our head telling us that we can’t do something, reply with a growth mindset approach and tell it that we can learn.
